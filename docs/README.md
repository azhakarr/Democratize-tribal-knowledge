## 🎯 OctoAcme Project Management Summary

### Overview & Core Principles

OctoAcme operates on a structured, iterative project lifecycle grounded in five core principles: customer-first prioritization, iterative delivery, clear ownership, data-informed decisions, and psychological safety. The organization employs a **lightweight governance model** that balances flexibility with accountability, moving projects through five distinct phases: **Initiation**, **Planning**, **Execution**, **Release**, and **Close & Retrospective**. Each project is anchored by a Project Manager (PM) who coordinates delivery and schedules, working alongside a Product Manager (PdM) who defines outcomes and success metrics. This dual leadership model ensures that execution stays aligned with business objectives and customer value.

### Key Roles, Artifacts & Communication Cadence

The framework recognizes three primary delivery personas—**Developers, Product Managers, and Project Managers**—each with distinct responsibilities and communication needs. Developers focus on implementing features to acceptance criteria while maintaining high test coverage; Product Managers own prioritization and outcome measurement; and Project Managers manage timelines, risks, and stakeholder alignment. Critical artifacts include the Project One-pager (for initiation), the prioritized backlog with acceptance criteria (for planning), a Risk Register (maintained throughout), and Release Notes (for deployment). Communication flows through a structured cadence: **weekly PM-PdM syncs, twice-weekly team standups, monthly stakeholder updates,** and ad-hoc escalations as needed. Status updates follow a consistent template covering progress, next steps, risks/blockers, and decisions required, ensuring transparency across all stakeholder groups.

### Execution, Quality & Risk Management

Day-to-day execution is tracked via a project board using columns (Backlog → Ready → In Progress → In Review → QA → Done), supported by daily 15-minute standups, weekly delivery syncs, and sprint-end demos. The team maintains quality through **unit tests, integration tests, end-to-end smoke tests, security scanning in CI, and manual QA when needed**. Pull requests are kept small (≤400 lines), include issue links and acceptance criteria, and require at least one approval before merging. Risk management is proactive: risks are identified during planning and ongoing execution, assessed for impact and likelihood, and monitored weekly with mitigation plans. **Blocker escalation follows a three-level path** (team triage → PM escalation → sponsor escalation), ensuring critical issues receive appropriate attention. This comprehensive approach to quality, transparency, and risk governance enables OctoAcme to deliver reliable increments while maintaining stakeholder confidence and team psychological safety.
